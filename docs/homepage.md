# Homepage Memory Capture — "One Question, One Thread"

The goal: in under 60 seconds, the homepage should take someone from "curious visitor" to "I just gave a piece of my life to this place, and I want to see what it does with it." No signup wall. No forms. Just a conversation.

## The Core Interaction: **The Single Prompt**

The entire hero is one thing: a soft, typewriter-style question fading in over a warm gradient.

> *"Tell me about the first time you felt truly loved."*

(Prompts rotate: *"What did your grandmother smell like?"* · *"What song was playing the day everything changed?"* · *"Who taught you how to be brave?"*)

Below it, two entry points — equal weight, no "primary" button:
- 🎙️ **Speak it** (tap-to-record, waveform animates as they talk)
- ✍️ **Type it** (expanding textarea, no character count, no pressure)

No "Sign Up." No "Get Started." Just the question.

## What Happens When They Answer

The moment they finish, the page **transforms, doesn't navigate**. Their words (transcribed if spoken) float up and settle into a card that looks like a page from a leather journal. Then three things happen in sequence — each one a tiny "whoa":

### 1. **Reflection** (2 seconds)
AI reads it back in one sentence that proves it *understood*:
> *"A summer afternoon, 1987, your dad teaching you to ride a bike on Maple Street — and the moment you realized he'd let go."*

This is the hook. The user thinks: *"It got it. It actually got it."*

### 2. **Expansion — The Thread** (the magic)
Three gentle follow-up cards fan out like a hand of playing cards, each one a doorway deeper into the memory:

- 🧵 **"What else happened that day?"** *(Narrative expansion)*
- 👥 **"Who else should remember this?"** *(Relational — seeds contact import)*
- 🎬 **"Want to see it come alive?"** *(AI-generates a soft visual/scene from their words)*

They can tap any one. Each tap deepens the memory and adds a node to a growing, visible **"memory thread"** at the bottom of the screen — a horizontal string of pearls, each pearl a moment they've shared.

### 3. **The Reveal — "Live On"** (the emotional payoff)
After 2–3 exchanges, the thread animates into a small constellation and the tagline softly appears:

> *"This is how you Live On."*
>
> *"You just gave us one memory. Imagine what a lifetime looks like."*
>
> **[Save my thread]** → *this* is the signup moment, and by now they want to.

## Why This Works

| Principle | How it shows up |
|---|---|
| **No friction first** | Zero fields before value. They experience the product before they "join" it. |
| **Show, don't tell** | The reflection + thread *demonstrates* what Yours Truly does better than any marketing copy. |
| **Emotional momentum** | Each interaction deepens investment. By the signup prompt, walking away means losing *their own* memory. |
| **The tagline earns itself** | "Live On" isn't slapped on — it appears exactly when the user *feels* it for the first time. |
| **Seeds every core feature** | The three follow-up cards preview: Timeline, Contacts/Collaboration, and AI Avatar — in one interaction. |

## The Subtle Details That Sell It

- **Ambient audio** — a faint piano note when their words land on the card. Almost subliminal.
- **Handwriting font** for their memory (not system UI font) — it feels personal, not corporate.
- **The cursor blinks in rhythm with a heartbeat** — 60bpm. Nobody notices consciously. Everybody feels it.
- **If they hesitate more than 10 seconds**, the prompt gently morphs: *"It's okay to start small. Even one word."*
- **On mobile**, the record button is thumb-sized and centered — designed for a grandmother, not a designer.
- **The thread persists in localStorage** — if they leave without signing up, coming back shows: *"Your memory is still here. Want to keep going?"*

## The One-Line Summary

**The homepage isn't a landing page — it's a first conversation with a listener who remembers everything.** The visitor arrives thinking they're evaluating an app, and leaves having already started their legacy.

That's how you turn *"what is this?"* into *"Live On."*
